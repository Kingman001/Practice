Our Breeds

  • Nigerian Indigenous Chicken (NIC)

    Description

    The NIC is a small-bodied chicken with a single comb, typically with mottled brown and black feathers.

    Characteristics

    They are known for their hardiness and ability to forage, thriving in various environments.

    Ecotypes

    The Fulani and Yoruba ecotypes are prominent examples, with the Fulani generally being larger.

    Productivity

    NICs are known for low egg production (40-50 eggs/year) and slow growth but excel in surviving on minimal feed.

  • Noiler Chicken

    Description

    A hybrid breed developed from the Nigerian Indigenous Chicken and the White Plymouth Rock, known for being dual-purpose (meat and eggs).

    Productivity

    They have better meat quality and growth rate compared to traditional indigenous breeds.

    Broiler Breeds

    Ross 308: Fast-growing broiler breed known for good performance and versatility.

    Cobb 500: Known for superior performance on lower-cost feed rations and uniformity for processing.

    Other Breeds: Marshall, Arbor Acre, and Red Broilers are also popular in Nigeria.
